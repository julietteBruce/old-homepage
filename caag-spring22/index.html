Seminar on Commutative Algebra and Algebraic Geometry - Spring 2022

For Spring 2022 the national Fellowship of the Ring Seminar will have talks every other Tuesday from 1:00pm - 2:30pm (PST). The Seminar on Commutative Algebra and Algebraic Geometry will occur on the weeks when there is no Fellowship of the Ring talk beginning on January 25th. The goal will be to have two 50-minute talks covering things related to commutative algebra and algebraic geometry.

Time: Alternating Tuesdays, 3:00pm - 5:00pm (PST)
Location: Evans Hall - Room #891 (In-Person)
Organizers: Juliette Bruce and David Eisenbud

This event is being organized by Juliette Bruce and David Eisenbud. If you have any questions about the seminar please email juliette.bruce 'at' berkeley.edu.

November 2, 2021 - Daniel Erman

One of the foundational results connecting syzygies with key algebraic geometry properties was Mark Green’s result on N_p conditions for smooth curves of high degree. A modern and streamlined proof of this result comes via Green’s Linear Syzygy Theorem (which had originally been conjectured by Eisenbud and Koh). I will discuss very recent work with Michael Brown which proves a Multigraded Linear Syzygy Theorem and uses this to obtain the first known examples of “virtual" N_p conditions for smooth curves of high degree in other toric varieties. This is joint work with Michael Brown.

November 16, 2021 - Ritvik Ramkumar

I will introduce the fiber-full scheme which can be seen as the parameter space that generalizes the Hilbert and Quot schemes by controlling the entire cohomological data. In particular, given a sequence of functions (h_0,..,h_n) the fiber-full scheme parameterizes subschemes X of P^n satisfying dim H^i(X,O_X(v)) = h_i(v). In this talk I will sketch the construction of the fiber-full scheme and describe some examples associated with classical Hilbert schemes. Time permitting, I will also discuss various future directions one can pursue. This is joint work with Yairon Cid-Ruiz.

November 23, 2021 - Lauren Cranton Heller

A Tate resolution for a sheaf F on projective space is a doubly infinite complex over an exterior algebra that encodes the cohomology of all twists of F. Its construction can be used to efficiently compute sheaf cohomology. Recently Michael Brown and Daniel Erman generalized this idea to general toric varieties. I will discuss their variation and its applications.

November 23, 2021 - Xianglong Ni

The Hilbert-Burch theorem is a structure theorem for resolutions of length 2. Is it the "best possible" structure theorem in this setting? In Hochster's 1974 CBMS talks, he proposed pursuing generic/universal resolutions for each resolution format (i.e. fixed sequence of Betti numbers), which would give a precise way of answering this type of question. The length 1 case is trivial and the length 2 case is fully understood, but the construction for length 3 is much more involved. I'll focus on some formats which are particularly nice from this standpoint and discuss some recent joint work with Lorenzo Guerrieri and Jerzy Weyman.

December 13, 2021 - Hannah Larson

The Chow ring of a variety packages information about its subvarieties and how they intersect (a sort of "algebraic version" of the cohomology ring). When studying Chow rings of moduli spaces, there are often certain "tautological" classes that arise naturally from the moduli problem. The Chow ring of the moduli space M_g of curves of genus g is known for g \leq 6 (Mumford, Faber, Izadi, Penev--Vakil). In each of these cases, the Chow ring is generated by tautological classes. We prove that the Chow ring of M_g is tautological for g = 7, 8, 9, thereby determining the Chow rings by earlier work of Faber. A key ingredient is our study of the Chow rings of Hurwitz spaces H_{k,g}, parametrizing degree k, genus g covers of P^1 for k \leq 5. This is joint work with Samir Canning.
