Seminar on Commutative Algebra and Algebraic Geometry - Spring 2022

For Spring 2022 the national Fellowship of the Ring Seminar will have talks every other Thursday from 3:00pm - 5:00pm (PST). The Seminar on Commutative Algebra and Algebraic Geometry will occur on the weeks when there is no Fellowship of the Ring talk beginning on January 27th. The goal will be to have two 50-minute talks covering things related to commutative algebra and algebraic geometry.

Time: Alternating Thursdays, 3:00pm - 5:00pm (PST)
Location: Zoom (Contact the organizers for link.)

January 27, 2022 - Hailong Dao

We introduce the notion of Burch ideals and Burch rings, notions inspired by a classical paper of Lindsay Burch. They are easy to define, and can be viewed as generalization of many well-known concepts, for example integrally closed ideals of finite colength and Cohen--Macaulay rings of minimal multiplicity. We give several characterizations of these objects. We show that they satisfy many interesting and desirable properties: ideal-theoretic, homological, categorical. The study of Burch rings also raises further surprising questions about nature of resolutions of modules, and if time permits I will discuss come of these new questions as well. The talk is based on joint work with Kobayashi-Takahashi and Eisenbud.

February 10, 2022 - Thomas Polstra

Abstract: Let \(R\) be a ring of prime characteristic \(p>0\). The ring \(R\) is non-singular if and only if the Frobenius morphism is flat. Therefore we label the singularities of $R$ by labeling the behavior of the Frobenius endomorphism. The oldest such ``\(F\)-singularity'' is the class of \(F\)-pure rings, which are rings for which the Frobenius morphism is a pure map. In this talk we consider the problem of determining necessary and sufficient conditions for when the homomorphic image of \(R\) is \(F\)-pure. This line of research has algebraic roots in a 1984 paper of Fedder, connections with the deformation of \(F\)-purity and \(F\)-regularity conjectures of tight closure theory, and is geometrically motivated by Kawakita's Inversion of Adjunction of Log Canonical Singularities Theorem from complex minimal model program. This talk is based on a collaboration with Austyn Simpson and Kevin Tucker.

February 10, 2022 - Josh Pollitz

In this talk we discuss homological aspects of certain homomorphisms between commutative noetherian rings. We focus on the class of quasi-complete intersection maps; these are at the center of a long standing conjecture of Quillen on the properties of the cotangent complex. The goal of this talk is to give an explicit description of the structure of the homotopy Lie algebra of a quasi-complete intersection map.This is joint work with Michael Debellevue.

February 24, 2022 - Bernd Sturmfels

We present joint work with Tobias Boege and Sonja Petrovic that builds on the 2005 PhD thesis of David Eisenbud's student George Kirkup. Rank one constraints on marginalizations of a tensor, given by a simplicial complex, can be made toric by a linear change of coordinates. We study these toric ideals, with emphasis on random graph models and independent set polytopes of matroids. We focus on the numerical algebra of parameter estimation, and we discuss various small models.

February 24, 2022 - Yassine El Maazouz

We study lines on smooth cubic surfaces over the field of p-adic numbers, from a theoretical and computational point of view. Segre showed that the possible counts of such lines are 0,1,2,3,5,7,9,15 or 27. We show that each of these counts is achieved. Probabilistic aspects are also investigated by sampling both p-adic and real cubic surfaces from different distributions and estimating the probability of each count. We link this to recent results on probabilistic enumerative geometry. Some experimental results on the Galois groups attached to p-adic cubic surfaces are also discussed.
