Seminar on Commutative Algebra and Algebraic Geometry - Fall 2021

For Fall 2021 the national Fellowship of the Ring Seminar will have talks every other Tuesday from 1:00pm - 2:30pm (PST). The Seminar on Commutative Algebra and Algebraic Geometry will occur on the weeks when there is no Fellowship of the Ring talk beginning on September 7th. The goal will be to have two 50-minute talks covering things related to commutative algebra and algebraic geometry.

Time: Alternating Tuesdays, 1:00pm - 3:00pm (PST)
Location: Evans Hall - Room #891 (In-Person)
Organizers: Juliette Bruce and David Eisenbud

September 7, 2021 - Hunter Spink

In this introductory talk, I will describe how algebraic geometers have extended foundational results on mixed volumes in convex geometry through a process called "Tropicalization", with special attention to the application of establishing log-concavity of sequences associated to graphs and hyperplane arrangements.

September 21, 2021 - Juliette Bruce

On projective space the Castelnuovo-Mumford regularity of a module can be characterized in terms of the minimal graded free resolutions of the module and its truncations. On more general toric varieties the analogous relationship between multigraded Castelnuovo-Mumford regularity and truncations does not hold. I will discuss recent work exploring the relationship between multigraded Castelnuovo-Mumford, truncations, Betti numbers, and virtual resolutions for modules on products of projective spaces.

September 21, 2021 - Kangjin Han

Let X in \(\mathbb{P}^{n+e}\) be any n-dimensional closed subscheme. In this talk, I will consider two notions related to syzygies: one is the property \(N_{d,p}\) (\(d\geq 2, p\geq 1\)), which means that X is d-regular up to p-th step in the minimal free resolution and the other is a new notion ‘ND(\(\ell\))' which generalizes the classical “being nondegenerate” to the condition that requires a general finite linear section not to be contained in any hypersurface of degree \(\ell\). First, as introducing condition ND(\(\ell\)) we'll regard examples and basic properties deduced from the notion. And I'll explain some consequences of these notions including sharp upper bounds on the graded Betti numbers of the first non-trivial strand of syzygies, which generalize results in the quadratic case to higher degree case. I will also introduce some open questions at the end of the talk.
